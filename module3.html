Module 3: Introduction to ESP32 and Electrical Circuits and Components.

In this session, we begun by being introduced to simple electrical components.
We learnt about Capacitor Resistor, Diode, Transistor, Inductor, Relay and Quartz Crystal.
Let me describe them below and how they work.

A capacitor is a passive two-terminal electrical component that can store energy in an electric field electrostatically.
When you apply a voltage over the two plates or connect them to a source, an electric field develops across the insulator, causing one plate to accumulate positive charge while negative charge gets collected on the other.

A resistor is a passive two-terminal electrical device that resists the flow of current.
It performs a vital function: controlling the voltage and the current in your circuit.

A diode is a two-terminal device that allows electric current to flow in only one direction.
When the cathode is heated by a filament, an invisible cloud of electrons, called space charge, forms in the vacuum. Though electrons are emitted from the cathode, the negative space charge repels them. As electrons canâ€™t reach the anode, no current flows through the circuit. However, when the anode is made positive, the space charge vanishes.

Transistors are tiny electronic components that control the flow of electricity in a circuit. Think of them like electronic switches or amplifiers.
A transistor is a small electronic component that works like a switch or an amplifier. It has three parts: the base, collector, and emitter. When a small current is applied to the base, it allows a much larger current to flow from the collector to the emitter. Without this base current, the transistor remains off, and no current flows between the collector and emitter.

An inductor, also known as a reactor, is a passive component of a circuit having two terminals.
Whenever the current passes through a wire, it creates a magnetic field. However, the unique shape of the inductor leads to the creation of a much stronger magnetic field.
